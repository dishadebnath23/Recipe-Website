Idli Sambhar

Idli sambar is a traditional South Indian dish comprising soft, fluffy idlis served with aromatic sambar, a flavorful lentil-based stew. Idlis are steamed rice and lentil cakes, while sambar is made by simmering pigeon peas (toor dal) with a variety of vegetables like onions, tomatoes, carrots, and drumsticks, along with a blend of spices like coriander, cumin, and tamarind pulp. The resulting sambar is rich, tangy, and fragrant, complementing the mild taste and spongy texture of idlis perfectly. This iconic dish is often enjoyed for breakfast or as a wholesome meal, representing the vibrant flavors of South Indian cuisine.

Here's a simple recipe for making Idli Sambhar:

Ingredients: For Idli:
2 cups idli batter (store-bought or homemade)
For Sambhar:
1 cup pigeon peas (toor dal), washed and soaked for 30 minutes
1 small onion, finely chopped
2 tomatoes, chopped
1 small carrot, chopped (optional)
1 small potato, diced (optional)
1 drumstick, cut into pieces (optional)
1 tablespoon sambar powder
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
2-3 dry red chilies
Few curry leaves
2 tablespoons oil
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:
For Idli:

  • Grease the idli molds with oil.
  • Pour the idli batter into the molds.
  • Steam the idlis in a steamer for about 10-12 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  • Once done, remove the idli molds from the steamer and let them cool slightly before unmolding the idlis. Set aside.

For Sambhar:
  1. Cook Pigeon Peas:
    • Drain the soaked pigeon peas and transfer them to a pressure cooker.
    • Add 2 cups of water, turmeric powder, and salt.
    • Pressure cook for about 4-5 whistles or until the dal is soft and mushy. Set aside.
  2. Prepare Sambar:
    • Heat oil in a large pan or kadhai over medium heat.
    • Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add cumin seeds, dry red chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté for a few seconds.
    • Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  3. Add Vegetables:
    • Add chopped tomatoes, carrots, potatoes, and drumstick (if using). Cook for a few minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened.
  4. Add Sambar Powder:
    • Stir in sambar powder and mix well. Cook for a minute to roast the spices.
  5. Combine Dal:
    • Add the cooked pigeon peas (toor dal) along with its cooking water to the pan. Mix well and let it simmer for about 5-7 minutes until the flavors meld together and the sambar thickens slightly.
  6. Seasoning:
    • Adjust salt to taste and add a pinch of asafoetida (hing) for flavor.
  7. Garnish and Serve:
    • Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves if desired.
    • Serve hot sambar with idlis.
Enjoy your delicious idli sambar! You can also serve it with coconut chutney for a complete South Indian breakfast experience.
